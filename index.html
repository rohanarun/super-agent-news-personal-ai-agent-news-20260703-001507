The market, right now

Gemini goes desktop

Google has brought its agentic assistant, Gemini Spark, to macOS, positioning it as a desktop‑aware agent that can work with local files and connect to multiple apps. This move signals how seriously Google is taking personal AI agents beyond the browser.

Source: techcrunch.com

Computer use becomes first‑class

In June 2026, Google made computer use a built‑in tool inside Gemini 3.5 Flash, underscoring a broader industry shift: agents are expected to see screens, click, type, and scroll — not just talk.

Source: rohitraj.tech

Reliability still lags ambition

Independent testing shows that today’s computer‑use agents often struggle with authentication and multi‑step workflows, requiring human supervision more than half the time.

Source: theeditorial.news

How the major players fit together

ChatGPT

A world‑class general assistant for writing, research, and reasoning. ChatGPT is evolving toward agents, but most users still experience it as a conversational tool.

Gemini

Google’s push into browser‑native and desktop‑aware agents is aggressive. Gemini Spark on Mac highlights a focus on cost‑efficient, high‑volume computer use.

Siri

Deeply embedded in Apple’s ecosystem and voice‑first by design. Recent coverage suggests steady progress, but Siri remains tightly scoped to Apple surfaces.

Grok

An opinionated assistant with real‑time and social context, increasingly visible through xAI. More personality than operator — at least for now.

Folk

A niche, CRM‑oriented tool that sits within the broader agent conversation as teams look for lightweight automation tied to relationships.

Orchids

An experimental entrant exploring agent‑driven automation. Representative of a long tail of builders testing what personal agents can become.
